Morning
Morning breathing exercises first thing can transform your energy levels and make you feel so much more awake.
Take a deep breath:
Through your nose, breathing deeply into your belly. Expand the breath to fill your chest and feel your rib cage expanding. As you breathe out feel the breath leaving the upper chest, then rib cage and finally the belly, letting them drop and relax.

4-7-8 breathing technique:
Exhale completely through the mouth, making a whooshing sound. Inhale through the nose for a count of 4 seconds. Hold your breath for 7. Exhale again for 8. Repeat 8 times.

Travelling
If you're a nervous traveller, the most anxiety-inducing moment of a trip can be the minutes before setting off.
Lengthen your breath:
Sit up straight in your seat and inhale deeply through your nose. Exhale slowly through your nose for two to three seconds longer than the amount of time it took to breathe in. Do this for 60 seconds and feel instantly calmer.
Workouts
Learning to breathe during workouts has benefits such as preventing dizziness during activity, improving athletic performance, and increasing fat burning.
To put it simply:
Always exhale on exertion and adopt a 3:2 inhale-to-exhale ratio; full inhales and full exhales. Many experts will say that this will fully oxygenate the muscles and clear the body of carbon dioxide.
